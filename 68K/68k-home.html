The Texas Instruments 68K Series

TI's "68K Series" includes graphing calculators with an implementation of the Motorola 68000 microprocessor.
Please note this is an unofficial naming scheme created by the TI fan community.
This includes:

  • TI-89, 89-Titanium
    •
  • TI-92, 92 II
  • TI-92 Plus, Voyage 200
These calculators are often praised for their CAS abilities. The CAS (Computer Algebra System) allows these calculators to handle unknown variables in a way the Z80 series never could.
TI only supports the 89-Titanium and Voyage 200 now, since the Nspire series has replaced it with similar functionality and more power.

They also have a plethora of programming tools and tutorials, some official, most not. You can learn more about your model's capabilities by visiting its specific page.
