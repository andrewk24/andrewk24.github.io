The TI-89 series

The TI-89 series is a miniturized version of the TI-92/Voyage 200 series. Like its parent, it is most well known for its Computer Algebra System, or CAS, which allows it to handle symbolic representation.

Hardware Revisions

In 1998, the original TI-89 released, being a TI-92 Plus in the form factor of a TI-83. It also removed the QWERTY keyboard to both decrease the size and for it to be allowed on the SAT and many other standardized tests.

In 2004, the updated TI-89 Titanium was released, which, similar to it's predecessor, was a smaller Voyage 200. It featured a new OS revision, a miniUSB port, and a shell very similar to that of the TI-84 Plus. This model is still supported by TI to this day.

Beyond the differences of the 89 and 89 Titanium, there were 4 hardware versions over the course of the TI-89's life.

  • HW1 - The initial release of the TI-89, @10MHz
  • HW2 - The initial release of the TI-89, @12MHz
  • HW3 - The initial release of the TI-89 Titanium, @12MHz
  • HW4 - The latest release of the TI-89 Titanium, @16MHz

The main difference between HW1 and HW2 is the new VRAM section for faster screen access, but this breaks compatibility with grayscale programs written for HW1, where 7-level grayscale was possible.

Later hardware revisions also support newer OSes, and larger limits on ASM programs. Early versions of the OS had no limit, and the latest OS has a limit of 64kb.
