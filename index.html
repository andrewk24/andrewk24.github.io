Welcome!

MATHEMADNESS is a site where we provide reviews, documentation, and resources to various graphing calculators. Usually, Texas Instruments, Casio, and HP are covered, but other noteworthy companies may be discussed as well.
The only requirement is that they can be programmed in some fashion!

TI-82 Advanced Python Edition Announced! • 4 March 2021

Exciting times! TI just announced a cost-reduced TI-83 Premium CE (French equivalent to the 84+CE) on their french site! It appears to be marketed as a cost-effective way to get into Python, and will be available in Fall of 2021. You can find a thread on Cemetech with more details here. (Hope it comes to America soon!)

arTIfiCE opens up Asm support after TI lockout • 29 October 2020

In TI's latest OS update for the 84 Plus CE and it's variants, support for unsigned programs written in eZ80 Assembly was removed.

However, a jailbreak arose just a few months later known as arTIfiCE! The jailbreak works by using a flaw in TI's Cabri Jr. Flash App, which work similarly to Asm programs, and can install launchers like Cesium into the apps menu without being signed. Huzzah!
Visit arTIfiCE's website to find out how to install it for yourself. Happy programming!
