The Texas Instruments Z80 Series

TI's "Z80 Series" includes graphing calculators with the Zilog Z80 and the similar Zilog eZ80 microprocessor.
Please note this is an unofficial naming scheme created by the TI fan community.
The calculators with regular Z80s include:

  • TI-73, 73-Explorer
  • TI-81
  • TI-82
  • TI-83, 83 Plus, 83 Plus Silver Edition
  • TI-84 Plus, 84 Plus Silver Edition, 84 Plus C Silver Edition
  • TI-85
  • TI-86
While the newer calculators with eZ80 chips include:
  • TI-84 Plus CE / 83 Premium CE
It's worth noting the TI-80 has a very similar UI to the Z80 models, and is often grouped in with them as such. However, it instead has a Toshiba T6M53 ASIC and is not compatible with other models outside of TI-BASIC.

These calculators are usually TI's most popular, one reason being their standard user inferface established in 1990.
Due to this lack of change for the past few decades, models as old as the TI-83 Plus from 1999 are still being sold and used to this day.

They also have a plethora of programming tools and tutorials, some official, most not. You can learn more about your model's capabilities by visiting its specific page.
