TI-84 Plus C Silver Edition

To experiment with color screens on their main-line Z80 calculators, Texas Instruments released the TI-84 Plus C Silver Edition in 2013. (84+CSE for short). To put it simply, it's an 84 Plus with a color screen, a rechargable battery to compensate for said screen, and an increase to 3.5MB of Archive or Flash. The OS has also been modified from the 84 Plus to handle the new screen type, and allows the user to graph over pictures loaded from a computer, and make the graph lines in different colors.

The issues arise with the fact that the 15 MHz Z80 found in the TI-84 Plus is not fast enough to handle smooth operation of the OS, especially when drawing to the screen. The new screen also means it is not natively compatible with older TI-84 Plus Assembly Programs, nor TI's own Flash applications. In response to this, TI released the TI-84 Plus CE, which did not fix compatibility, but rather, made it it's own platform, with a much faster CPU and slimmer design.
