TI-81

Introduced in 1990, the TI-81 was Texas Instrument's first graphing calculator. It wasn't the first on t he market, that honor goes to Casio's fx-7000G 4 years earlier. As well as competing with Casio, it also served as a follow-up to TI's line of pocket computers, offering similar functionality at a lower price and size.

However, it's remarkable how little has changed from this time-tested design. It features Parametric and Standard graphing, matricies, all while being a very competent scientific calculator. It does all this with the "Equation Operating System", which, modified, of course, still is in use on modern monochrome TI graphers.
