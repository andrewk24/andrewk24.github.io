TI-83 Series

The TI-83 is the longest running model of calculator that Texas Instruments still sells. Originally released in 1996 as a follow-up to the TI-82, it introduced a slew of new graphing modes, having polar, parametric, and sequence graphing, and a much improved TI-Basic.

In 1999, the TI-83 Plus was introduced, adding 160KB of rewritable flash, (named by TI as "Archive"), and shrunk the RAM due to address space limitations. This is the best selling 83 model, and the only one TI still sells.

Finally, in 2001, the TI-83 Plus Silver Edition was released, and quickly discontinued. It featured a 3x Clock speed boost, almost 10x flash, and over 4x more RAM. However, most of the extra RAM was not available to the user. It also featured a silver transluscent case with glitter.
